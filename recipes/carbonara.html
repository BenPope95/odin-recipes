Spaghetti Carbonara

Spaghetti Carbonara is an italian dish made with Spaghetti tossed in a creamy sauce made from eggs, pancetta, and parmasean cheese.

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 1 pound pancetta diced
  • 1 pound Spaghetti
  • 3 large eggs
  • 10 tablespoons grated Parmasean or Pecorino Romano cheese
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat; add pancetta. Cook, turning occasionally, until evenly browned and crispy, 5 to 10 minutes. Remove from heat and drain on paper towels.
  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti in the boiling water, stirring occasionally until tender yet firm to the bite, about 9 minutes. Drain and return to the pot. Let cool, stirring occasionally, about 5 minutes.
  3. Whisk eggs, 1/2 of the Parmasean Pecorino Romano cheese, and some black pepper in a bowl until smooth and creamy. Pour egg mixture over pasta, stirring quickly, until creamy and slightly cooled. Stir in pancetta. Top with remaining cheese and more black pepper.
